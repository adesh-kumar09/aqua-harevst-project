What is Commercial Rainwater Harvesting?

Commercial rainwater harvesting involves collecting rainwater runoff from large roof areas (like office buildings, warehouses, factories, retail centers) and storing it in sizable tanks for non-potable use within the business operations.

It's a strategic approach for businesses to secure a supplementary water source, significantly reduce reliance on municipal water, lower operating costs, and demonstrate environmental leadership.

Why It Matters for Your Business

Beyond water collection, implementing RWH offers significant operational, financial, and reputational advantages for commercial properties.

Common Commercial Systems

Above-Ground Tanks

Large capacity tanks (steel, poly, fiberglass) collecting from extensive roof areas via 'dry' or 'wet' conveyance. Often includes advanced pre-filtration.

High Capacity & Accessible
Underground Storage

Utilizes large concrete vaults or modular plastic tanks buried beneath parking lots or landscaped areas. Ideal for space-constrained sites.

Space Saving & Invisible
Integrated Systems

Sophisticated setups with automated pumps, advanced filtration/treatment (UV, etc.), and integration with building management systems for optimized use.

Commercial Applications for Harvested Water

Toilet & Urinal Flushing

Major water consumer in commercial buildings; significant savings potential.

Landscape Irrigation

Watering lawns, gardens, and green roofs on corporate campuses or retail sites.

Cooling Tower Makeup

Supplementing water for HVAC cooling towers (requires appropriate treatment).

Vehicle & Equipment Washing

Washing company fleets, machinery, or facility exteriors.

Process Water

Use in certain industrial processes (subject to water quality needs and treatment).

Fire Suppression Reserves

Maintaining water levels in dedicated fire protection tanks.

*Note: Specific uses depend on water quality requirements, necessary treatment levels, and local regulations.

Implementing Your Commercial System

  1. Site Assessment & Feasibility
    Analyze roof area, rainfall data, water demand, site constraints, regulations, and ROI potential.
  2. System Design & Engineering
    Professional design of conveyance, filtration, storage, treatment, and distribution components. Obtain permits.
  3. Install Collection & Conveyance
    Ensure large-scale gutters, downspouts, and pipes are correctly installed and sized.
  4. Implement Filtration & Treatment
    Install robust pre-filters (vortex, basket) and any required post-storage treatment (sediment, carbon, UV).
  5. Install Storage & Pumps
    Position/bury tanks on engineered foundations. Install pumps, controls, and distribution plumbing.
  6. Commissioning & Maintenance Plan
    Test the system thoroughly. Establish a clear schedule for professional inspection and maintenance.
