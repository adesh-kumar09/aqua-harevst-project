What is Residential Rainwater Harvesting?

Simply put, residential rainwater harvesting is the process of collecting the rain that falls onto your home's roof and storing it in tanks or barrels for later use.

Instead of letting valuable rainwater run off into storm drains, you capture it directly at the source. This collected water can then be used for various non-drinking purposes around your house and garden, helping you conserve tap water and reduce your environmental impact.

Why It Matters for Your Home

Implementing rainwater harvesting offers tangible benefits beyond just collecting water. It's a proactive step towards water independence and environmental responsibility.

Reduces Water Bills

Lower your reliance on municipal water, especially for non-potable uses like gardening and cleaning.

Supports Sustainability

Conserve precious groundwater and reduce the energy needed for water treatment and transport.

Provides Water Security

Gain a backup water source during droughts, water restrictions, or emergencies.

Common Residential Systems

Rain Barrels

The simplest setup. Barrels (50-100 gallons) placed under downspouts collect water, typically used for manual garden watering via a spigot.

Simple & Affordable
Dry System

Pipes run directly from gutters to a storage tank located nearby. Pipes empty completely after rain, preventing freezing issues in colder climates.

Efficient & Common
Wet System

Uses underground pipes connecting multiple downspouts to a tank often located further away. Pipes retain water between rains.

Setting Up Your System: Key Steps

  1. Assess & Plan
    Calculate roof area, check local rainfall, determine water needs, and check regulations. Choose a system type.
  2. Install Gutters & Downspouts
    Ensure they are clean, correctly sloped, and sized for your roof area. Add leaf guards.
  3. Set Up Pre-Filtration
    Install first-flush diverters and/or screen filters before the tank inlet to remove debris.
  4. Position & Connect Storage Tank
    Place the tank(s) on a stable, level base. Connect downspouts/pipes securely to the tank inlet.
  5. Install Overflow & Distribution
    Ensure an overflow pipe directs excess water away safely. Add a tap or connect a pump for distribution.
  6. Maintain Regularly
    Clean gutters, filters, and tanks periodically to ensure water quality and system efficiency.

Regular maintenance is key. This includes:
